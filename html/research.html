Electrocatalysis

Electrochemical CO2 Reduction to Sustainable Fuels

My work focuses on the selective reduction of CO2 into high-value hydrocarbons and syngas. By stabilizing Cu(I) species in oxidative microenvironments on engineered MXene surfaces, we've achieved unprecedented control over C-C coupling reactions.

"Suppressed HER while accelerating C-C Coupling using Ti3C2Tx MXene interfacial layers."

Nitrogen Fixation

Sustainable Ammonia Synthesis — Beyond Haber-Bosch

Decentralized ammonia production via N2RR is the key to sustainable agriculture. We investigate Ti2C MXene basal plane activation and surface termination engineering to lower the activation barrier for N≡N bond cleavage at room temperature.

"MXene surface terminations unlock N2 activation pathways previously thought impossible at room temperature."

Fundamentals

MXene/Water Interface: A Descriptor-Driven Approach

Understanding the potential of zero charge (PZC) and electron transfer coefficients (β) allows for a predictive model of catalyst performance. This fundamental approach bridges the gap between theoretical DFT predictions and experimental reality.

"First electrochemical descriptor framework applied to MXene surfaces for predicting reaction kinetics."
